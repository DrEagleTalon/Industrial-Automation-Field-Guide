## Description

This guide covers various types of electrical conduit and fittings used in industrial installations, detailing applications, advantages, and proper installation techniques to ensure safe and compliant wiring.

## Core Concepts

### What is it?

Electrical conduit protects wiring from damage and provides pathways for wire routing. Fittings connect sections and ensure structural integrity and environmental protection.

### How it works

Conduits and fittings create enclosed channels that protect wiring against mechanical impacts, moisture, corrosion, and chemical exposure, enhancing electrical safety and system longevity.

### When to Use

Use conduit in all industrial applications where wiring requires physical protection, environmental resilience, or compliance with local electrical codes.

## Specifications & Parameters

- **Rigid Metal Conduit (RMC):** Galvanized steel, robust, suitable for hazardous or outdoor areas.
    
- **Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT):** Thinner wall, lighter, easy to install indoors.
    
- **Flexible Metal Conduit (FMC):** Used for tight bends and vibration isolation.
    
- **PVC Conduit:** Non-metallic, corrosion-resistant, ideal for underground or wet locations.

|Symptom|Likely Cause|Diagnostic Step|Tool(s) Needed|
|---|---|---|---|
|Wire damage|Improper conduit fitting|Inspect and correct fitting|Visual inspection, multimeter|
|Moisture intrusion|Wrong conduit type used|Verify conduit rating|Visual inspection|

## Field Checklist

-  Select appropriate conduit type.
    
-  Use proper fittings and connectors.
    
-  Confirm conduit pathways comply with local codes.
    
-  Inspect regularly for integrity and damage.
